# Tenant Fees Act 2019 – Permitted and Prohibited Tenant Charges

## Overview

The Tenant Fees Act 2019 bans most letting fees charged to tenants in the private rented sector in England.

The legislation applies to:

* new tenancy agreements signed on or after 1 June 2019
* renewed tenancy agreements from 1 June 2019

The purpose of the Act is to reduce upfront and ongoing tenancy costs and improve transparency by preventing hidden fees.

Under the Act, landlords and letting agents may only charge tenants for specific permitted payments.

Any payment not expressly permitted by the legislation is prohibited.

---

# Permitted Payments

From 1 June 2019, landlords and letting agents may only require tenants to make the following payments in relation to a tenancy.

## Rent

Rent remains a permitted payment.

The legislation aims to ensure advertised rent reflects the true cost of the tenancy without hidden charges.

---

## Tenancy Deposit

A refundable tenancy deposit is permitted, subject to statutory caps.

### Deposit Caps

* maximum 5 weeks’ rent where annual rent is below £50,000
* maximum 6 weeks’ rent where annual rent is £50,000 or above

The deposit must comply with tenancy deposit protection legislation where applicable.

---

## Holding Deposit

A refundable holding deposit may be charged to reserve a property.

The holding deposit must not exceed:

* 1 week’s rent

---

## Payments for Tenant-Requested Changes

Landlords and agents may charge for:

* variation of a tenancy
* assignment of a tenancy
* novation of a tenancy

These charges are capped at:

* £50
* or reasonable higher costs if properly evidenced

---

## Early Termination Fees

Payments associated with ending the tenancy early are permitted where the termination is requested by the tenant.

---

## Utility and Household Payments

Tenants may still be required to pay:

* utilities
* communication services
* TV licence
* Council Tax

where required under the tenancy agreement.

---

## Default Fees

Default fees are only permitted in limited circumstances specified in the tenancy agreement.

Permitted default fees include:

* late payment of rent
* replacement of lost keys or security devices

---

# Prohibited Fees

Any payment not specifically authorised by the Tenant Fees Act is prohibited.

Examples of prohibited fees can include:

* administration fees
* referencing fees
* inventory fees
* check-in or check-out fees
* contract renewal fees
* viewing fees
* guarantor fees

Landlords and letting agents cannot require tenants to make prohibited payments as a condition of granting, renewing, or continuing a tenancy.

---

# Administration Fee Restrictions

The Act significantly restricts administration charges to tenants.

General administrative costs connected with:

* setting up the tenancy
* preparing agreements
* conducting references
* arranging inventories
* renewing contracts

must normally be paid by the landlord or letting agent rather than the tenant.

The legislation is intended to ensure the party contracting the service — usually the landlord — bears the cost of that service.

---

# Enforcement

Local enforcement authorities are primarily responsible for enforcing the Tenant Fees Act.

An independent lead enforcement authority was created to support local authorities and provide guidance regarding enforcement.

Bristol City Council was appointed as the lead enforcement authority for lettings enforcement under the Act.

---

# Tenant Rights

Tenants have the right to challenge prohibited fees.

Where a prohibited payment has been charged, tenants may:

* refuse to pay the prohibited fee
* seek repayment of prohibited payments already made
* report landlords or agents to local enforcement authorities

A landlord or letting agent may face enforcement action for breaching the legislation.

---

# Relationship With Deposit Protection

The Tenant Fees Act works alongside tenancy deposit protection legislation.

Although tenancy deposits remain permitted, statutory caps now limit the amount that can be requested.

Deposit disputes remain subject to tenancy deposit protection scheme rules and adjudication procedures where applicable.

---

# Key Compliance Principles

## Only Permitted Payments May Be Charged

Any tenant payment outside the statutory list is prohibited.

## Fees Must Be Transparent

The legislation aims to remove hidden tenancy costs and improve pricing clarity.

## Landlords Bear Service Costs

Where landlords instruct agents or services for tenancy administration, the landlord is generally responsible for those costs rather than the tenant.

---

# Relevant Legal Context

The Tenant Fees Act 2019 applies to the private rented sector in England and forms part of broader consumer protection and tenancy regulation reforms designed to improve fairness and affordability for tenants.
