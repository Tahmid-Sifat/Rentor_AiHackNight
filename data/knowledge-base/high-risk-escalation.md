# High-Risk Situations — When to Escalate

## This Tool's Limits

Deposit Defender AI is designed to help with standard tenancy deposit disputes. Some situations go beyond a deposit dispute and require professional legal help, specialist housing charities, or urgent action.

## When to Contact Citizens Advice Immediately

- You have received a Section 21 (no-fault eviction) notice and are unsure if it is valid
- You have received a Section 8 (fault-based eviction) notice
- You are being threatened with eviction or asked to leave immediately
- Your landlord has entered the property without notice or permission
- You are being harassed or intimidated by the landlord or their agents
- Your landlord has changed the locks or removed your belongings

## When to Contact Shelter

Shelter is a housing charity offering free specialist advice:

- You are at risk of homelessness
- You have been illegally evicted
- You are experiencing harassment from your landlord
- You need urgent housing advice
- The deposit dispute involves a significant amount of money affecting your housing

## Illegal Eviction

Changing locks, removing belongings, switching off utilities, or physically intimidating a tenant to leave is illegal. This is a criminal offence under the Protection from Eviction Act 1977.

If you are experiencing illegal eviction:
- Call the police if you are in immediate danger
- Contact your local council's housing department (they have emergency powers)
- Contact Shelter or Citizens Advice

## Unprotected Deposit

If your deposit was not protected in an approved scheme within 30 days of receipt, this is a serious legal issue that goes beyond a standard dispute:

- The landlord may be liable to pay you 1-3 times the deposit as a penalty
- Any Section 21 notice served while the deposit is unprotected is invalid
- You may need to apply to the County Court to recover the deposit and penalty

This situation requires separate legal advice or assistance from Citizens Advice.

## Court Proceedings

If:
- The landlord has applied to court for possession
- You have received court papers
- A court date has been set

You need immediate legal advice. Court deadlines are strict. Contact:
- Citizens Advice
- Your local council housing duty officer
- Legal Aid (if eligible)
- A solicitor

## Discrimination

If you believe you are being treated differently because of a protected characteristic (race, sex, disability, religion, sexual orientation, age, pregnancy, etc.), this may be unlawful under the Equality Act 2010. Contact Citizens Advice or a solicitor.

## Very Large Financial Claims

If the total amount in dispute exceeds £1,000-2,000, or represents a significant portion of your financial situation, consider:

- Getting independent legal advice
- Using Citizens Advice for a case assessment
- Considering small claims court if ADR is not available or fails

- **Citizens Advice**: 0800 144 8848 (England) | 0800 702 2020 (Wales)
- **Shelter**: 0808 800 4444 (England) | 0808 800 4444 (Scotland)
- **Shelter Webchat**: shelter.org.uk
- **Gov.uk deposit schemes**: gov.uk/tenancy-deposit-protection

## Deposit Defender AI Disclaimer

This tool helps tenants organise evidence and prepare dispute communications. It does not:
- Provide legal advice
- Replace a solicitor, Citizens Advice, Shelter, or your deposit protection scheme
- Guarantee any outcome
- Represent you in any legal or ADR proceeding

Always verify guidance with a qualified professional before taking legal action.
