# Evidence Checklist for Deposit Disputes

## Tenant's Key Documents

### Essential Documents
- [ ] Signed tenancy agreement (including any clauses about cleaning, damage, deposit)
- [ ] Deposit protection certificate or Prescribed Information document
- [ ] Check-in inventory (signed or unsigned)
- [ ] Check-out report (if provided)
- [ ] Any emails or messages from landlord or agent about the deposit

### Supporting Evidence
- [ ] Photos taken at check-in (with timestamps if possible)
- [ ] Photos taken at check-out (with timestamps)
- [ ] Copy of any cleaning invoices from end of tenancy
- [ ] Receipts for any repairs made before leaving
- [ ] Communications about repairs during the tenancy (shows landlord was aware of issues)

## What to Ask the Landlord For

For each deduction, ask:

### For Cleaning Charges
- Evidence property was professionally cleaned at start of tenancy
- Itemised cleaning invoice with company name, date, and specific tasks
- Check-in vs. check-out comparison photos
- Statement of what specific cleaning was required beyond the check-in standard

### For Damage Claims
- Dated photos of the alleged damage
- Repair invoice or quote with itemised breakdown
- Evidence that the damage was not present at check-in
- Justification for cost, especially for replacements (age of item, expected lifespan)

### For Repainting
- Photos of the damage requiring repainting
- Invoice from decorator
- Evidence of how the damage occurred
- Explanation of why normal scuffs do not constitute fair wear and tear in this case

### For Replacement Items
- Age of item at start of tenancy
- Evidence of condition at check-in
- Invoice for replacement
- Justification for full replacement rather than repair

## Evaluating Evidence Strength

### Strong Tenant Evidence (disputes deduction)
- Check-in inventory showing same/similar condition
- Landlord's photos that actually show the same or worse condition was present at check-in
- No professional cleaning invoice from check-in (counters cleaning charge)
- Tenant Fees Act prohibition applies (e.g., mandatory clean clause)
- Long tenancy (more fair wear and tear expected)
- No signed check-out report from tenant
- No dated photos from landlord

### Weak Tenant Position (deduction may be valid)
- Check-out report clearly showing damage not in check-in inventory
- Dated photos clearly showing new damage
- Signed check-out report
- Tenant admits to the damage in writing
- Professional invoices for work done

## Key Questions to Answer

1. Was the deposit protected in an approved scheme within 30 days?
2. Was Prescribed Information provided?
3. Was there a signed check-in inventory?
4. What condition was the property in at check-in?
5. What specific damage is claimed and is it proven with photos?
6. Are the charges proportionate to actual loss?
7. Does fair wear and tear apply to any of the charges?
8. Are any charges prohibited under the Tenant Fees Act?
9. Has the landlord provided invoices or just estimates?
10. Is the tenancy still within the ADR window (not all schemes have a time limit, but check)?
