# Deposit Protection Overview

## Legal Requirement

In England and Wales, landlords must protect tenancy deposits in a government-approved scheme within 30 days of receiving the deposit. The three approved schemes are:

- **Deposit Protection Service (DPS)** - custodial and insured options
- **MyDeposits** - insured scheme
- **Tenancy Deposit Scheme (TDS)** - custodial and insured options

## What Must Be Protected

Any deposit for an Assured Shorthold Tenancy (AST) that started on or after 6 April 2007 must be protected. This includes:

- Cash deposits
- Debit/credit card payments used as deposit
- Deposits held by letting agents on the landlord's behalf

## Prescribed Information

Within 30 days of receiving the deposit, the landlord must also provide the tenant with "Prescribed Information" including:

- Name and contact details of the scheme
- How to apply to have the deposit returned
- What to do if there is a dispute

## Consequences of Non-Compliance

If a landlord fails to protect a deposit or provide Prescribed Information, the tenant can apply to court and may receive between 1x and 3x the deposit amount as a penalty, in addition to the return of the deposit itself.

An unprotected deposit also prevents the landlord from serving a valid Section 21 notice.

## Checking Deposit Protection

Tenants can check if their deposit is protected using the free checking tools on each scheme's website. You will need:

- Your tenancy start date
- Your deposit amount
- Your postcode or the landlord/agent's postcode

## Key Tenant Rights

- Tenant is entitled to know which scheme protects the deposit
- Tenant must receive Prescribed Information
- Deposit can only be deducted for specific reasons set out in the tenancy agreement
- Deposit must be returned within 10 days of agreement on the amount

## Dispute Resolution

All three schemes offer free Alternative Dispute Resolution (ADR) if landlord and tenant cannot agree. The ADR process is:

1. Tenant and landlord cannot agree on deductions
2. Either party applies to the scheme's ADR service
3. Both parties submit evidence to an independent adjudicator
4. Adjudicator makes a binding decision
5. Deposit is distributed according to the decision

The tenant should NOT agree to release any disputed portion while the dispute is ongoing.
