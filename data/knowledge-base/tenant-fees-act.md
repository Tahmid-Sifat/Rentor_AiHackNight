# Tenant Fees Act 2019

## Overview

The Tenant Fees Act 2019 came into force on 1 June 2019. It bans most fees that landlords and letting agents can charge tenants in England. Similar legislation applies in Wales (Renting Homes (Fees etc.) (Wales) Act 2019) and Scotland (Tenant Fees Act — Scotland has had a ban since 2012).

## Permitted Payments Only

Landlords and agents can only charge tenants for:

1. **Rent** — the agreed monthly rent
2. **Deposit** — capped at 5 weeks' rent (or 6 weeks if annual rent exceeds £50,000)
3. **Holding deposit** — capped at 1 week's rent, refundable in most cases
4. **Changes to tenancy** — capped at £50 (or reasonable costs if higher) for tenant-requested changes like adding a permitted occupier
5. **Early termination** — only if the tenant requests it, and costs must not exceed landlord's losses
6. **Default fees** — only if explicitly stated in the tenancy agreement:
   - **Lost keys** — reasonable costs for replacement
   - **Late rent** — only after rent is 14 days overdue, capped at 3% above Bank of England base rate

## What Is Banned

The following fees are prohibited:

- Admin fees for setting up or renewing a tenancy
- Inventory fees
- Check-in and check-out fees
- Cleaning fees as a condition of the tenancy (i.e. requiring professional clean at end)
- Reference check fees
- Credit check fees
- Professional cleaning clauses (mandatory end-of-tenancy cleaning)
- Gardening fees not in the agreement
- General administration charges
- Fee for renewing a tenancy

## Mandatory Cleaning Clauses

A clause requiring the tenant to pay for professional cleaning at the end of the tenancy regardless of condition is now a prohibited payment under the Tenant Fees Act 2019. Such clauses cannot be enforced.

However, if the property is left dirtier than it was received, the landlord can still claim for the actual cost of cleaning required — this is about actual damage/loss, not a mandatory fee.

## Consequences of Charging Prohibited Fees

- Any prohibited payment must be returned to the tenant within 28 days
- Trading Standards can impose a fine of up to £5,000 for a first offence
- Subsequent offences can result in a fine of up to £30,000 or a banning order
- A landlord who has charged a prohibited payment cannot serve a valid Section 21 notice until the payment has been returned

## Deposit Cap

Since 1 June 2019, deposits are capped at:
- **5 weeks' rent** for properties with annual rent under £50,000
- **6 weeks' rent** for properties with annual rent of £50,000 or more

Any deposit taken above this cap must be returned. If the landlord refuses, the tenant can apply to the First-tier Tribunal.

## Admin Fees Specifically

Charging an "admin fee" for organising contractors or processing deductions from the deposit is not a permitted payment. A landlord cannot deduct an admin fee from the deposit unless:

- It relates to a genuine loss incurred from early termination requested by the tenant
- It is a default fee explicitly permitted in the agreement and compliant with the Act

An admin fee for "arranging contractors" is not permitted and is challengeable.
