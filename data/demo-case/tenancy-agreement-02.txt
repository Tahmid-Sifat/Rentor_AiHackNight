## 1. The Property

The Landlord agrees to let to the Tenant the furnished property known as:

**Flat 6, 42 Netherby Street, Fulham, London SW6 3BW**

together with all fixtures, fittings, furniture, white goods and contents listed within the Inventory and Schedule of Condition.

The Property comprises a one-bedroom furnished flat situated on the third floor of the building.

---

## 2. Term of Tenancy

2.1 The tenancy shall commence on:

**01 February 2025**

and shall continue for a fixed term of:

**12 months**

ending on:

**31 January 2026**

2.2 Following expiry of the fixed term the tenancy may continue as a statutory periodic tenancy unless terminated in accordance with this Agreement.

---

## 3. Rent

3.1 The rent shall be:

**£1,975.00 per calendar month**

payable in advance on or before the 1st day of each month.

3.2 Rent shall be paid by standing order to a bank account nominated by the Landlord or Managing Agent.

3.3 The first rental payment must clear before keys are released.

3.4 If rent remains unpaid for more than 14 days after becoming due, interest may be charged at 3% above the Bank of England base rate until payment is made.

3.5 Repeated late payment may constitute a breach of tenancy obligations.

---

## 4. Tenancy Deposit

4.1 The Tenant shall pay a tenancy deposit in the sum of:

**£2,275.00**

before commencement of the tenancy.

4.2 The deposit shall be protected within a government authorised tenancy deposit protection scheme in accordance with the Housing Act 2004.

4.3 The deposit may be used towards:

* unpaid rent
* damages beyond fair wear and tear
* missing items
* cleaning costs where necessary
* unpaid utility charges
* locksmith or replacement key costs
* breach of obligations under this Agreement

4.4 Evidence supporting proposed deductions may include:

* invoices
* contractor quotations
* check-in/check-out reports
* photographs
* correspondence records
* inventory comparisons

4.5 The Tenant shall be provided with Prescribed Information relating to the tenancy deposit scheme within the statutory time period.

---

## 5. Use of the Property

5.1 The Property shall be occupied solely as a private residence by the Tenant only.

5.2 The Tenant shall not assign, sublet, licence or share occupation of the Property without prior written consent from the Landlord.

5.3 No illegal or anti-social activity shall take place at the Property.

5.4 The Tenant shall not cause nuisance or disturbance to neighbouring occupiers.

---

## 6. Inventory and Condition

6.1 An Inventory and Schedule of Condition will be prepared at the start of the tenancy and provided to the Tenant.

6.2 The Tenant shall check the inventory carefully and notify the Landlord or Agent in writing of any inaccuracies within seven (7) days of moving into the Property.

6.3 In the absence of written objections the Inventory shall be treated as accepted by the Tenant.

6.4 The Inventory may contain written descriptions, photographs, appliance serial numbers, meter readings and cleanliness assessments.

6.5 The check-out inspection shall be compared against the original Inventory and Schedule of Condition.

---

## 7. Tenant Obligations

The Tenant agrees:

7.1 To keep the interior of the Property in a reasonably clean and tidy condition during the tenancy.

7.2 To maintain the furnishings, fixtures and appliances in good condition and not intentionally or negligently damage them.

7.3 To promptly report any maintenance issue, leak, defect or repair requirement to the Landlord or Managing Agent.

7.4 To adequately ventilate the Property and take reasonable steps to prevent condensation, mould or mildew build-up.

7.5 To replace light bulbs, smoke detector batteries where applicable, and maintain ordinary housekeeping standards.

7.6 Not to affix adhesive hooks, nails or fixtures to walls without permission.

7.7 Not to redecorate the Property without prior written consent.

7.8 To permit access upon at least 24 hours notice for inspections, repairs or maintenance, except in emergency situations.

---

## 8. Cleaning Requirements

8.1 The Tenant shall return the Property at the end of the tenancy in substantially the same standard of cleanliness as recorded within the check-in inventory, allowing for fair wear and tear.

8.2 Particular attention should be given to:

* oven and extractor fan cleaning
* bathroom limescale
* kitchen surfaces and cupboards
* carpets and flooring
* internal windows
* upholstery and furnishings

8.3 Where the Property was professionally cleaned immediately prior to occupation, the Landlord may reasonably expect the Property to be cleaned to a comparable standard upon checkout.

8.4 The Tenant is not automatically required to use a professional cleaning company, however where cleaning falls below the standard recorded at check-in, reasonable cleaning costs may be deducted from the deposit.

8.5 Any personal belongings, rubbish or food items left at the Property following the tenancy may incur disposal charges.

---

## 9. Damages and Fair Wear and Tear

9.1 The Tenant shall not be responsible for fair wear and tear resulting from ordinary and reasonable use of the Property.

9.2 Fair wear and tear shall take account of:

* length of tenancy
* age and quality of items
* normal usage
* number of occupants

9.3 The Tenant may be held responsible for:

* stains beyond normal use
* broken furniture or fittings
* burns, tears or significant marks
* missing inventory items
* unauthorised alterations
* damage caused by negligence or misuse

9.4 The Landlord shall not seek betterment and deductions shall reflect reasonable depreciation and age of items.

---

## 10. Repairs and Maintenance

10.1 The Landlord shall remain responsible for maintaining:

* structure and exterior
* heating and hot water systems
* electrical installations
* plumbing and sanitation systems

10.2 The Tenant shall not attempt repairs to electrical, gas or plumbing systems other than minor everyday maintenance.

10.3 The Tenant shall take reasonable care to avoid blocked drains caused by grease, wipes or foreign objects.

10.4 Damage caused by the Tenant’s misuse or negligence may be charged to the Tenant.

---

## 11. Utilities and Outgoings

11.1 The Tenant shall be responsible for:

* gas
* electricity
* water charges
* internet and media services
* council tax

for the duration of the tenancy.

11.2 Utility accounts should be transferred into the Tenant’s name where appropriate.

11.3 Evidence of unpaid utility charges identified after the tenancy may be used to support deductions from the deposit.

---

## 12. Check-Out Procedure

12.1 The Tenant shall vacate the Property by 11:00am on the final day of the tenancy unless otherwise agreed.

12.2 All keys, entry fobs and security devices must be returned at checkout.

12.3 A final inspection may take place after the Tenant vacates.

12.4 The Tenant is encouraged to attend the check-out inspection where possible.

12.5 Photographs may be taken during checkout for evidential purposes.

---

## 13. Notices

13.1 Any notice sent under this Agreement may be delivered by hand, email or first class post.

13.2 Notices addressed to the Tenant may be served at the Property address during the tenancy.

---
