# ASSURED SHORTHOLD TENANCY AGREEMENT

(Housing Act 1988 as amended by the Housing Act 1996)

This Agreement is made on the 12th day of August 2024 between:

**Landlord:**
Mr Daniel Whitmore
Correspondence Address: 14 Hanover Court, Islington, London N1 2TT

and

**Tenant:**
Ms Olivia Bennett
Current Address Prior to Tenancy: 7 Flat 3, Oakfield Road, Croydon CR0 2HB

---

## 1. Property

The Landlord agrees to let and the Tenant agrees to take the property known as:

**Flat 4B, 118 Ashcombe Road, Clapham, London SW4 9TR**

together with the fixtures, furnishings and effects listed in the Inventory and Schedule of Condition attached to this Agreement.

The property is a second-floor furnished one-bedroom flat including shared access to communal hallway areas and refuse facilities.

---

## 2. Term of Tenancy

The tenancy shall be for a fixed term of:

**12 months**

commencing on:

**01 September 2024**

and ending on:

**31 August 2025**

Subject to earlier termination in accordance with this Agreement.

---

## 3. Rent

3.1 The Tenant shall pay rent of:

**£1,850.00 (One Thousand Eight Hundred and Fifty Pounds)** per calendar month.

3.2 Rent shall be payable monthly in advance on the 1st day of each month by standing order to an account nominated by the Landlord.

3.3 The first month’s rent shall be paid prior to the commencement of the tenancy.

3.4 If the rent is unpaid for more than 14 days after becoming due, the Landlord reserves the right to charge interest at 3% above the Bank of England base rate on the outstanding amount.

3.5 Persistent late payment may be treated as a breach of tenancy obligations.

---

## 4. Tenancy Deposit

4.1 The Tenant shall pay a tenancy deposit of:

**£2,130.00**

prior to occupation of the Property.

4.2 The deposit shall be held against damages, unpaid rent, cleaning costs, missing items, unpaid utility charges or breaches of the Tenant obligations contained within this Agreement.

4.3 The deposit shall be protected in accordance with the requirements of the Housing Act 2004 within an authorised Tenancy Deposit Protection Scheme.

4.4 Prescribed information relating to the protection of the deposit shall be provided to the Tenant within the statutory time limits.

4.5 Deductions may be proposed at the end of the tenancy where supported by evidence including invoices, inventory reports, photographs or contractor quotations.

---

## 5. Use of Property

5.1 The Property shall be occupied solely as a private residential dwelling by the Tenant only.

5.2 The Tenant shall not assign, sublet, take in lodgers, or part with possession of the Property without the prior written consent of the Landlord.

5.3 No business shall be carried out at the Property.

5.4 The Tenant shall not cause nuisance, annoyance or disturbance to neighbouring occupiers.

---

## 6. Tenant Obligations

The Tenant agrees:

6.1 To keep the interior of the Property in a clean and tidy condition throughout the tenancy.

6.2 To maintain reasonable standards of housekeeping and hygiene.

6.3 To promptly notify the Landlord of any maintenance issue, leak, fault or damage affecting the Property.

6.4 To take reasonable precautions against condensation and damp including adequate ventilation of rooms.

6.5 Not to damage walls, floors, furniture, appliances or fixtures beyond fair wear and tear.

6.6 To replace batteries in smoke alarms where appropriate during the tenancy.

6.7 To dispose of refuse in accordance with local council collection arrangements.

6.8 Not to smoke inside the Property.

6.9 Not to keep pets at the Property without written permission.

---

## 7. Cleaning Obligations

7.1 The Tenant shall keep the Property reasonably clean during the tenancy term.

7.2 At the end of the tenancy the Property must be returned in substantially the same condition of cleanliness as recorded in the Inventory and Check-In Report, allowing for fair wear and tear.

7.3 Particular attention should be paid to:

* kitchen appliances
* bathroom limescale
* flooring and carpets
* windows internally
* removal of personal belongings and rubbish

7.4 Professional cleaning is not mandatory unless the Property was professionally cleaned immediately prior to the tenancy commencement and evidence of this has been supplied.

7.5 Where excessive cleaning is required following checkout, reasonable cleaning costs may be deducted from the deposit.

---

## 8. Inventory and Check-In

8.1 An Inventory and Schedule of Condition shall be prepared at the start of the tenancy.

8.2 The Tenant shall check the inventory carefully and notify the Landlord or Agent of any discrepancies within 7 days of moving into the Property.

8.3 Failure to notify discrepancies may result in the inventory being treated as accepted by the Tenant.

8.4 The Inventory may include photographs, appliance records, meter readings and condition notes.

---

## 9. Maintenance and Repairs

9.1 The Landlord shall remain responsible for:

* structural repairs
* exterior and roof
* plumbing installations
* heating and hot water systems
* electrical wiring and installations

9.2 The Tenant shall remain responsible for:

* replacing light bulbs where accessible
* maintaining ordinary cleanliness
* preventing avoidable damage
* reporting repairs in reasonable time

9.3 The Tenant shall not attempt repairs to gas or electrical systems.

9.4 The Tenant shall permit reasonable access upon at least 24 hours notice for inspection, maintenance or repair works except in emergency situations.

---

## 10. Fair Wear and Tear

10.1 The Landlord acknowledges that deterioration caused by normal everyday use of the Property and its contents constitutes fair wear and tear.

10.2 Charges shall not be made for reasonable ageing of furniture, carpets, paintwork or appliances arising from ordinary use.

10.3 Consideration shall be given to the length of tenancy, quality and age of items, and number of occupants.

---

## 11. Utilities and Council Tax

11.1 The Tenant shall be responsible for payment of:

* gas
* electricity
* water charges
* broadband and television services
* council tax

during the tenancy period unless otherwise agreed in writing.

11.2 The Tenant agrees to place accounts into their own name where required.

11.3 Any unpaid utility charges identified after the tenancy may be deducted from the deposit where evidence is available.

---

## 12. Check-Out Procedure

12.1 At the end of the tenancy the Tenant shall:

* vacate the Property by 12:00 noon on the final day
* return all keys, fobs and access devices
* remove all personal possessions
* leave the Property reasonably clean and tidy

12.2 A check-out inspection may be conducted against the original inventory and schedule of condition.

12.3 The Tenant is encouraged to attend the checkout inspection where possible.

12.4 Missing keys may result in charges for replacement locks if security is compromised.

---

## 13. Notices

13.1 Any notice sent under this Agreement may be delivered by hand, email or first class post.

13.2 Notices to the Tenant may be served at the Property address.

---

## 14. Data and Communication

The Landlord or Agent may retain contact information for tenancy management, repairs, legal obligations and deposit administration.

---

## 15. Ending the Tenancy

15.1 The tenancy shall end on expiry of the fixed term unless renewed or continued as a statutory periodic tenancy.

15.2 The Tenant remains liable for rent until keys are returned and vacant possession is provided.

15.3 Any abandonment of the Property shall not automatically end the tenancy.

---
